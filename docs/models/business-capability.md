# Business Capability Model

The business capability model describes **what** UCL does, independent of how it is organised or what technology supports it. Capabilities are stable over time even as organisational structures and systems change.

## Level 0 — Enterprise

```{mermaid}
graph TD
    UCL[UCL] --> ED[Education]
    UCL --> RS[Research]
    UCL --> PS[Professional Services]
    UCL --> EN[Engagement]
```

## Level 1 — Capability Domains

### Education

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Programme Design | Design and approval of academic programmes |
| Teaching Delivery | Delivery of teaching activities |
| Assessment & Feedback | Student assessment and feedback processes |
| Student Lifecycle | Admissions, enrolment, progression, graduation |

### Research

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Research Strategy | Setting research priorities and direction |
| Grant Management | Pre- and post-award grant administration |
| Research Data Management | Storage, curation, and sharing of research data |
| Research Output | Publication, dissemination, and impact tracking |

### Professional Services

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Finance | Financial planning, accounting, and reporting |
| Human Resources | Recruitment, development, and workforce management |
| Estates & Facilities | Campus management and space planning |
| IT Services | Technology provision and support |

### Engagement

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Alumni Relations | Engagement with UCL alumni community |
| Partnerships | Industry and institutional partnerships |
| Public Engagement | Outreach and public-facing activities |
| Communications | Internal and external communications |
